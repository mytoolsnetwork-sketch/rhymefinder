Types of Rhymes Explained

  • Perfect rhymes — words that end with the exact same sound (e.g. cat / hat / flat).
  • Near rhymes — words that almost rhyme, sharing similar but not identical sounds (e.g. orange / porridge). Also called slant rhymes or half rhymes.
  • Synonyms — words with the same or similar meaning, useful when you need to swap a word while keeping the idea.

Tips for Writing Better Rhymes

  • Don't force a rhyme — if the perfect rhyme sounds unnatural, a near rhyme often flows better.
  • Try multisyllabic rhymes — rhyming two or three syllables creates a more sophisticated and satisfying effect.
  • Use synonyms — if you can't find a good rhyme for your word, find a synonym and rhyme that instead.
  • Read it aloud — the best test for any rhyme is how it sounds when spoken or sung.

Frequently Asked Questions

What words rhyme with "love"?

Perfect rhymes for "love" include above, dove, glove, shove. Near rhymes include move, groove, prove, soothe — great for song lyrics where a perfect rhyme feels too rigid.

What words rhyme with "orange"?

"Orange" is famously difficult to rhyme in English. Near rhymes include porridge, forage, storage. Rappers often use multisyllabic constructions to work around it creatively.
